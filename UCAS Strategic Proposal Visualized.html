1. Executive Summary

1.1. Project Overview

This proposal represents a crucial strategic investment to support the University College of Applied Sciences (UCAS)'s position as an "Innovation Leader." It directly aims to enhance **International and Institutional Partnerships (Academic & Research Partnerships)** by updating and developing our English electronic platform (en.ucas.edu.ps). The project will standardize the User Experience (UX/UI) across 11 main sections, ranging from Academic Deanships to Research Centers, achieving maximum **professionalism** and **high technical quality** to serve the international cooperation agenda. Leveraging the internal efficiency of the Deanship of Engineering and Smart Systems, this project is expected to be fully completed within an **accelerated timeframe of only 14 days**.

2. Problem Statement and Challenge

Despite our academic distinction, our current English interface poses a risk to our ability to attract international partners and presents a challenge to joint collaboration:

  • Weak Global Institutional Appearance: The current design does not reflect UCAS's standing as a credible international academic or research partner, hindering the building of relationships with other universities.
  • Inconsistent User Experience: There is a disparity in formatting and content across the 11 sections, making it difficult for international academics and institutions to quickly access details of our research programs and academic plans.
  • Lack of Clarity in Competency Display: Academic staff profiles and expertise are not displayed professionally, which would otherwise facilitate cooperation opportunities and leverage their expertise for other universities.

4. Proposed Solution and Strategic Pillars

The project will be executed based on three strategic pillars to ensure a final product that serves institutional goals:

4.1. Pillar One: Technical Quality and Professionalism (Engineering Excellence) on WordPress Platform

I guarantee that this pillar will receive the highest priority, focusing on the structural architecture of the WordPress platform:

  • Theme Engineering: Building all modifications and customizations through a **dedicated Child Theme**. This ensures that no code or design changes are lost when the main WordPress theme is updated, guaranteeing long-term site sustainability.
  • Future Scalability: Designing the core WordPress architecture to accommodate future content growth (increasing the number of sections or programs) without requiring comprehensive re-engineering.
  • Performance and Caching Optimization: Implementing techniques to improve WordPress page loading speed, including asset compression, database query optimization, and activating caching best practices.
  • Dynamic Content Structure: Utilizing Custom Post Types (CPTs) and custom fields to organize data for deanships, programs, and staff profiles in an organized and segregated manner.
  • Technical Compliance: Building interfaces using the latest programming standards to ensure sustainability and ease of future maintenance.

4.2. Pillar Two: Design and User Experience (UX/UI Modernization)

  • Unified and Responsive Design: Adopting a **"Mobile-First" approach** to ensure a seamless experience on all devices.
  • Integrated Visual Identity: Unifying fonts, colors, and data presentation style across all 11 sections.
  • Accessibility (WCAG Compliance): Ensuring that pages comply with Web Content Accessibility Guidelines to serve users with disabilities.

4.3. Pillar Three: Strategic Content, Clarity, and Human Capital for International Cooperation

4.3.1. Content Completion and Data Acquisition Requirement

A critical requirement for the full completion of this project and populating all necessary pages (11 sections) is **obtaining sufficient and reliable input and content**. Therefore, effective and direct communication with deanships and college specialists to provide detailed information for each page, including goals, programs, and staff profiles, is an essential condition for adhering to the specified timeframe (**14 days**). **To this end, a Standardized Template will be sent to each deanship to populate the required data for its page, with a strict Hard Deadline of no more than 48 hours to ensure work flow continuity and adherence to the 14-day schedule.**

  • Content Refinement: Reviewing all English texts to ensure accuracy, clarity, and suitability for the audience of **partner universities and research institutions**.
  • Highlighting Human Capital for Cooperation: **Coordinating with deanships to compile profiles of distinguished faculty and staff, including a summary of their research specialization and any previous international experience, to facilitate cooperation and leverage their expertise in developing joint academic plans.**
  • Search Engine Optimization (SEO): Implementing SEO best practices to increase UCAS's visibility in international academic and research search results.
